## Course Description

Interdisciplinary introduction to entrepreneurship that covers the foundational frameworks and skills involved with managing the creation and growth of new businesses as well as the social structures that shape accessibility of entrepreneurship as a career path. 

This course addresses three important areas related to new ventures:
1. **The Social Foundations of Entrepreneurship**
2. **Business Model Development**
3. **Critical Entrepreneurial Management Skills**

---

## Enrollment Requirements

- Open only to business majors

---

## Course Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

1. **Understand Social Foundations**: Analyze how social structures, networks, and cultural contexts shape entrepreneurial opportunities and accessibility
2. **Develop Business Models**: Design and evaluate viable business models using contemporary frameworks and tools
3. **Apply Critical Skills**: Demonstrate essential entrepreneurial management skills including opportunity recognition, resource acquisition, and venture validation
4. **Foster Creativity & Innovation**: Generate innovative solutions to business challenges through design thinking and creative problem-solving (TOI1)
5. **Evaluate Ventures**: Critically assess new venture ideas using evidence-based frameworks
6. **Navigate Uncertainty**: Make strategic decisions under conditions of uncertainty and resource constraints

## Learning Environment

This course emphasizes **experiential learning** and **creative problem-solving** (TOI1). Students will:

- Engage in design thinking workshops
- Participate in customer discovery interviews
- Develop innovative business solutions
- Present and defend venture concepts
- Provide constructive peer feedback

### Creativity & Innovation Focus (TOI1)

Throughout the course, students will develop their creative capabilities through:

1. **Design Thinking Exercises**: Weekly activities that promote divergent and convergent thinking
2. **Innovation Challenges**: Real-world problems requiring creative solutions
3. **Iterative Development**: Embracing failure as a learning tool
4. **Cross-disciplinary Collaboration**: Working with diverse team members
5. **Expression Through Pitching**: Communicating innovative ideas effectively
